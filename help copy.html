Understanding the Kanban Board

The Kanban board is designed to visualize the work process and improve task management. It consists of different columns representing various stages of work. Typically, these columns include:

  • To-Do: Tasks that need to be started.
  • In Progress: Tasks currently being worked on.
  • Await Feedback: Tasks that require input or approval from others.
  • Done: Completed tasks.

By moving tasks across these columns, teams can easily track progress, identify bottlenecks, and ensure a smooth workflow. Join offers an intuitive Kanban board that makes task management seamless and effective.

Creating and Assigning Tasks

With Join, you can easily create tasks and assign them to team members. Here's how you can do it:

  • Create a Task: Click on the "Add Task" button to create a new task. Enter the task details such as title, description, and category.
  • Assign to Users: Assign the task to one or more team members. This ensures that everyone knows their responsibilities.
  • Set a Deadline: Specify a deadline for the task to ensure timely completion.
  • Categorize Tasks: Organize tasks by categories to streamline your workflow and make it easier to find specific tasks.

Managing Subtasks

For larger tasks that require multiple steps, Join allows you to create subtasks. This helps in breaking down complex tasks into manageable pieces, ensuring that every aspect of the task is covered. To add subtasks:

  • Add Subtasks: Within a task, click on "Add Subtask" and fill in the details.
  • Track Progress: Subtasks can be individually tracked, providing a clear view of the overall task progress.
